Borscht

The Greatest Soup You Will Ever Taste

Julia Hablak (Bryn Mawr College 2021) and Zoé Hommerich (Davidson Collge 2023) both study Russian and enjoy culture, food and cooking. This Russian-loving pair wanted to share an easy recipe for borscht that anyone can follow! Although borscht originally originated from modern day Ukraine, the ingredients used for an authentic Russian borscht are very different from the ingredients used in the original Ukrainian soup. Until the late 17th century, the soup spread around Slavic countries while being modified to reflect regional crops as well as the tastes of the regional nobility. By the end of the 17th century, the name borscht was simply used as an umbrella term for a series of similar sour tasting soups. The first version of red Russian borscht, adopting its bright red colouring from added beetroot, is believed to have been be created by Ukrainians living under Russian rule in the early 18th century. Potateos and tomatoes were added during the the early 19th century through Western European influence. For more information on who really owns borscht, click here .

Preparation time

less than 30 minutes

Cooking time

30 min to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4-6

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 15g butter
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stick, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 medium sized beetroots (around 450g unpeeled weight), peeled and diced
  • 1 large waxy potato, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2½ pints good quality beef stock
  • ½ purple cabbage, finely shredded
  • 2 tomatoes, skinned, cored and chopped
  • salt
  • freshly ground black pepper

To Serve

  • sour cream
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped dill
  • bread of your choosing

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil and butter in a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan. Once melted add the carrot, celery and onion and cook gently for a few minutes. Add the beetroot, potato and garlic. Fry for a couple of minutes, until well coated and combined.
  2. Add the stock and season with salt and black pepper. Bring to just below the boil and then simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Add the cabbage and tomatoes. Cover and leave to simmer for a further 20 minutes.
  4. Serve the soup with dollops of sour cream and a sprinkling of dill
